# Conduct an After Action Review (AAR)

1. Schedule an After Action Review (AAR) meeting within **72 hours** 
   and invite the following attendees:
   - The Incident Commander
   - IT Security Lead
   - Service owners involved in the incident
   - Key responders and forensics analysts
   - MHA Liaison (if government data was involved)

2. Designate an AAR owner who will investigate the incident in advance 
   of the meeting, reviewing notes, logs, and reports to prepare 
   a structured summary.

---

## Conduct the AAR Meeting

Document answers to the following key questions:

1. **What happened?** Create a timeline supported by data or artifacts. 
   Avoid blame. Find facts.

2. **What was supposed to happen?**
   - Detail deviations from process, procedure, or best practice
   - Identify ways the incident could have been detected sooner or 
     responded to more effectively

3. **What were the root causes?** Find root cause to things that 
   happened and to things that should have happened.

4. **How can we improve?** Capture action items with assignees and 
   due dates. Consider:
   - Stop: what should we stop doing?
   - Start: what should we start doing?
   - Continue: what should we keep doing?

---

## Communicate AAR Status and Results

The AAR owner, in coordination with the IT Security Lead and MHA 
Liaison where applicable, will communicate the status of the AAR 
to relevant stakeholders.

| Status | Description |
|---|---|
| Draft | AAR investigation is still ongoing |
| In Review | AAR completed, ready to be reviewed in the meeting |
| Reviewed | AAR meeting done, content reviewed and agreed upon |
| Closed | No further actions needed, documentation finalised |

Communicate results internally and finalise all AAR documentation 
within **5 working days** of the incident closure.
